# Contact List

A decentralized contact manager that allows users to save and tag wallet addresses with notes, reputation scores, and categories. Contacts are stored locally or on-chain/[IPFS](https://ipfs.tech/), and could include verification status. Useful for DAOs, communities, or marketplaces where trusted contributor tracking is essential. This tool should be easily integratable into other apps so that many apps can share the same contact list. We should be able to have a contact log of all our own addresses and purposes and all of our friends, and businesses contacts addresses.

![Contact List mockup](/images/project/contact-list.jpg)

## General Description

[TrustList](https://trustlist.monark.io) is a decentralized contact system for Web3 users, allowing wallets to organize and annotate their interactions with other users on the blockchain. In traditional applications, contact lists are centralized, opaque, and often context-specific (e.g., friends on social media, teammates in Slack). TrustList brings this functionality into the decentralized space, enabling individuals and DAOs to create wallet-linked directories enhanced with user-defined metadata.

Each user can create and store contact labels such as "developer," "trusted partner," "frequent collaborator," or "flagged for spam." These tags can be stored off-chain in encrypted form or published publicly via decentralized storage. Optional features include contact scoring, endorsement systems, or timestamped interaction logs.

This system is highly relevant in DAOs, bounty platforms, and decentralized marketplaces where recurring interactions and trust relationships develop over time but aren't formally recorded. It bridges the gap between the anonymity of blockchain and the reputational needs of professional coordination. Students working on this project will explore Web3 UX, encrypted metadata storage, decentralized identity standards, and human-friendly interfaces for wallet-based interactions. While simple at its core, TrustList teaches foundational skills around identity layering, privacy models, and interoperability across dApps.

## Deliverables & Desired Functionalities

- Interface web utilisant [Next.js](http://next.js/), [TypeScript](https://www.typescriptlang.org), [shadcn-ui](https://ui.shadcn.com/), [tailwindcss](https://tailwindcss.com)
    - Créer une interface avec fonction de recherche pour parcourir, étiqueter et gérer les contacts, et permettre aux utilisateurs de filtrer les contacts par catégorie, rôle ou niveau de confiance.
- Authentification utilisateur
    - Activer l'inscription et la connexion via portefeuille en utilisant Web3Auth par MetaMask.
- Système d'étiquetage pour les adresses de portefeuille
    - Étiqueter les adresses avec des labels comme “partenaire”, “client”, “arnaque”, ou même des noms complets.
- Champs de métadonnées éditables pour notes, rôles et historique
    - Ajouter des notes, un historique de contact ou des journaux d'interaction par portefeuille.
- Stockage chiffré hors-chaîne ou public sur la chaîne
    - Stocker les listes de contacts localement, de façon chiffrée, ou via on-chain/IPFS.
- (Optionnel) Système de score de réputation ou d'endossement, pouvant être défini manuellement par l'utilisateur au départ
